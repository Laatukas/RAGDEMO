Electronics engineering is a specialized branch of electrical engineering focusing on active components (semiconductors, transistors, integrated circuits) to design, develop, and test systems like computers, communication tools, and robots. It deals with "light current" applications—such as signal processing, embedded systems, and consumer electronics. 

Key Aspects of Electronics Engineering
Definition & Scope: A sub-discipline of electrical engineering, it emerged to manage circuits utilizing active components for controlling electric current flow.
Core Areas: Key fields include analog/digital electronics, telecommunications, robotics, control systems, and embedded systems.
Job Functions: Electronics engineers design, manufacture, test, and maintain equipment, including creating technical diagrams and ensuring safety compliance.
Distinction from Electrical Engineering: While electrical engineering often deals with "heavy current" (power generation), electronics engineering focuses on "light current" for data and signal processing.
Key Skills: Proficiency in mathematics, physics, computer programming, circuit design, and simulation software. 

Typical Applications
Consumer Electronics: Cell phones, computers, and home appliances.
Systems Engineering: Automation, robotics, and medical instrumentation.
Communication: Radio, satellite, and networking hardware.
Component Design: Development of microcontrollers, integrated circuits, and sensors.
